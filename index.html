ORBIT-Surgical: An Open Simulation Framework for Accelerated Learning in Surgical Autonomy

Anonymous Author(s)

Abstract

Physics-based simulations have the potential to rapidly accelerate progress in robotic manipulation and locomotion across a wide variety of tasks. However, a fast, accurate, and robust surgical simulation environment remains elusive. In this paper, we present ORBIT-Surgical, a physics-based surgical robot simulation framework using Isaac ORBIT using the Intuitive Surgical da Vinci Research Kit (dVRK) robotic surgical assistant. We provide twelve benchmark environments for the dVRK which represent various common tasks in surgical training. ORBIT-Surgical leverages GPU parallelization to train reinforcement learning (RL) and imitation learning (IL) algorithms to perform long-horizon surgical robotics tasks. ORBIT-Surgical also facilitates hand-scripted and human-expert data collection, reducing barriers to real-world implementations of IL methods. We evaluate ORBIT-Surgical sim-to-real transfer of the trained policies on a real world dVRK platform to ensure transferability. ORBIT-Surgical provides an open-source benchmark for learning complex and long-horizon surgical subtasks.
